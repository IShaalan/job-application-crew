# Product Manager Role Pack -- Battle-tested from 95+ real applications

This role pack captures positioning strategies, keyword priorities, and learnings from real PM applications across multiple role subtypes. Updated automatically during the retrospective phase.

---

## How to Use This File

When preparing a new application:
1. Identify the role subtype from the categories below
2. Review successful strategies for similar roles
3. Adapt the approach for the specific company/role

## Role Subtype: AI Product Manager

### What They're Looking For
- Deep understanding of ML/AI capabilities and limitations
- Ability to translate technical complexity into user value
- Experience shipping AI-powered features (not just POCs)
- Data-driven decision making with model performance metrics
- Comfort with ambiguity and experimentation

### CRITICAL: AI PM is still a PM role
Don't sacrifice core PM skills for technical AI depth. Every AI PM resume must show BOTH:

**Core PM Skills Checklist (MUST include):**
- [ ] Cross-functional leadership: engineering, design, data science, GTM, leadership
- [ ] Customer discovery and research
- [ ] Prioritization and decision-making
- [ ] Stakeholder communication and transparency
- [ ] Roadmap ownership and execution

**AI/ML Skills (layer on top of core PM):**
- [ ] Data science partnership
- [ ] Model monitoring and evaluation
- [ ] AI/ML technical understanding
- [ ] Responsible AI practices

### Positioning Angles
1. **"ML-fluent PM"**: Emphasize ability to speak the language of data scientists while keeping user needs central
2. **"Ship AI features, not experiments"**: Focus on production AI, not just POCs -- show monitoring, evaluation, and iteration
3. **"Chatbot to agentic workflow"**: If you've moved from reactive AI (chatbots) to proactive AI (embedded workflows), this distinction is powerful
4. **"Responsible AI advocate"**: Highlight human-in-the-loop design, guardrails, quality monitoring

### Keywords
- Machine learning, AI, LLM, NLP, computer vision (as relevant)
- Agentic workflows, model monitoring, model evaluation
- A/B testing, experimentation, data pipeline
- Responsible AI, fairness, bias mitigation, human-in-the-loop
- Cross-functional, data science, ML engineering

### Summary Template
```
Senior Product Manager with X years building [B2B/B2C] products, including Y years
shipping AI-powered features. Track record of cross-functional leadership across
engineering, design, data science, and GTM. Combines customer discovery with
technical depth to prioritize high-impact AI features that deliver measurable
business outcomes.
```

### Battle-tested Learnings

**What works:**
- Opening cover letter with company-specific data point (e.g., "90% of 2025 sales from new products") lands stronger than generic mission references
- Framing domain-specific AI work in neutral language (e.g., "users" not domain jargon, "document-heavy domain" not industry name) lets architecture stories transfer across industries
- Mapping each experience bullet to a specific pillar in the JD shows deliberate targeting
- Naming specific monitoring tools (Langfuse, W&B, MLflow) demonstrates operational AI maturity
- Builder/side projects (MCP servers, agentic workflows) signal active experimentation with agent frameworks

**What fails:**
- Over-indexing on AI/ML technical skills while missing core PM competencies
- Using "chatbot" generically when the work was actually agentic workflows
- Listing "model monitoring" in skills without a bullet demonstrating it
- Leading with the wrong story type (technical architecture for a UX-focused role, or vice versa)

### Keywords

- Developer experience, documentation

## Role Subtype: Senior/Lead Product Manager

### What They're Looking For
- Strategic thinking beyond individual features
- Stakeholder management at executive level
- Mentoring and team development
- Business impact and revenue accountability
- Vision setting and roadmap ownership

### Positioning Angles
1. **"Strategic product leader"**: Think beyond features to business outcomes and market positioning
2. **"Builder of PM teams"**: Experience growing and mentoring PMs
3. **"Executive influencer"**: Can drive alignment at senior levels through structured communication

### Keywords
- Product strategy, vision, roadmap
- Executive communication, board presentations
- OKRs, business metrics, P&L
- Team leadership, mentoring, hiring
- Stakeholder management, cross-functional alignment

### Battle-tested Learnings

**Title level ambiguity:**
- When JD lists "Senior / Principal" or leaves level undecided, default to "Senior" for ATS safety unless the candidate explicitly requests "Principal"
- Inflated titles signal "overqualified" -- match the posting exactly

## Role Subtype: Platform / Infrastructure PM

### What They're Looking For
- Experience with internal tools and platforms
- Developer empathy and UX for technical users
- API design and documentation quality
- Migration and adoption strategy
- Measuring internal productivity (not vanity metrics)

### Positioning Angles
1. **"Internal customer champion"**: Treat internal teams like external customers with the same rigor
2. **"Force multiplier"**: Show how platform work enables company velocity, not just team efficiency
3. **"Migration expert"**: Successfully moved teams to new platforms with high adoption and low friction

### Keywords
- Platform, infrastructure, internal tools
- Developer experience, API design
- Adoption, migration, change management
- Productivity metrics, developer velocity
- Self-service, documentation, SDKs

### Battle-tested Learnings

**Summary positioning:**
- Lead with the HM's mental model, not your strongest card. For a platform PM role, leading with AI expertise is wrong even when that's the most recent work
- JD-exact language in summary earns ATS points. Mirror "integration-heavy," "operations and GTM intersect" verbatim (not paraphrased)
- AI as secondary signal, not lead: For non-AI-PM roles, AI expertise belongs in the last summary sentence as forward-looking depth ("for where platforms are headed")

**Cover letter hook:**
- Don't prescribe strategy to the company. Opening with "The most important thing [Company] can build is..." is presumptuous
- Open with insight from lived experience. The hook should say something only someone who has done this work would say
- State the role name in the opening. A recruiter picking up the letter cold should not have to infer it
- Sales Engineering background is underused for platform/integration roles -- most PMs haven't been on the enterprise technical sales side

### Keywords

## Role Subtype: Startup Generalist PM

### What They're Looking For
- Breadth and versatility across product, marketing, and operations
- Ownership mentality and scrappiness
- Speed over perfection
- Direct customer contact and rapid feedback loops
- Wearing multiple hats (product + marketing + content ops, etc.)

### Positioning Angles
1. **"Full-stack PM"**: Can do product, product marketing, and growth in one seat
2. **"0-to-1 builder"**: Track record of launching products from scratch
3. **"Customer-obsessed operator"**: Ship fast, learn from users, iterate

### Keywords
- 0-to-1, MVP, product-market fit
- Full-stack product management
- Customer development, lean startup
- Growth, acquisition, retention
- Product marketing, positioning, GTM

### Battle-tested Learnings

**3-in-1 startup PM roles:**
- Roles spanning product + product marketing + content ops need all three lanes demonstrated in bullets. Missing any lane = HM concern.
- Map explicitly in strategy doc which bullets cover which lane.

**Unknown company problem:**
- For companies HMs won't recognize, rely on Key Achievements to establish scale ($XM pipeline, XM users) rather than company descriptors in headers
- The achievements do the job without requiring parenthetical explanations

**Rare skills get more real estate:**
- If a role explicitly requires a rare skill (e.g., ASO), it must appear in summary + Key Achievements + Skills, not just the skills section

## Company Type Positioning

### Startup (Seed - Series B)
- Emphasize breadth of experience
- Show ability to operate with ambiguity
- Highlight startup experience or startup-like conditions
- Demonstrate customer obsession
- Show you can execute without large teams

### Growth Stage (Series C+)
- Show experience scaling products/teams
- Highlight process improvements you've driven
- Demonstrate strategic thinking with execution
- Emphasize cross-functional collaboration
- Show business impact at scale

### Big Tech / Enterprise
- Show experience with complex stakeholder landscapes
- Highlight long-running initiatives and patience
- Demonstrate structured thinking and documentation
- Emphasize metrics and business cases
- Show experience with compliance/process

## Cross-Cutting Learnings

### Summary Writing Formula
**3-sentence formula**: S1 = what you do + context (not keywords) | S2 = differentiating methodology, one coherent idea, JD-mirrored, no colon-separated noun dump | S3 = credentials + scale evidence

**Noun dump failure mode**: S2 like "owns X: tool1, tool2, tool3, tool4" is wrong. One idea, written as a sentence.

### Keyword Tiering
- **Tier 1 (Must nail)**: Core requirements repeated throughout JD, in title, or in required qualifications
- **Tier 2 (Should include)**: Mentioned multiple times but not primary focus
- **Tier 3 (OK to skip)**: Examples in lists, nice-to-haves, one-time mentions
- Prioritize Tier 1 coverage over forcing Tier 3 keywords awkwardly

### Cover Letter Rules
- Open with insight from lived experience, not generic enthusiasm
- "genuinely" = red flag for AI writing. Lead with the insight, not enthusiasm about the insight
- State the role name explicitly in the opening paragraph
- Main story: add the "why the decision was made" texture the resume bullet can't show
- 250-350 words, 3-4 paragraphs
- No em-dashes anywhere (they are the #1 AI-writing signal)

### Domain Translation
When applying outside your primary domain, translate all domain-specific terms:
| Domain-specific term | Domain-neutral replacement |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| domain users | users |
| domain end-users | end users |
| domain workflow | workflow context |
| specialized domain | regulated domain / document-heavy domain |
| domain-scale deployment | enterprise deployment |
| Expert-in-the-Loop | human-in-the-loop |
| domain quality drift | quality drift |
